## Tutor Feedback

The feedback was broadly positive: the report was recognised for strong theoretical grounding in BDI and multi-agent architectures, clear structure, good use of literature, and consistent academic integrity.

Three areas were identified for development. First, greater technical depth on implementation mechanisms and runtime behaviour. This resonated: the report was a design proposal so implementation detail was deliberately constrained, but I had built the system and the gap the tutor identified is directly what the individual presentation (Assignment 2) addresses. Second, deeper comparison with alternative agent architectures, something I'll carry into the dissertation, where comparative analysis is more central. Third, stronger synthesis of literature to support critical argumentation rather than using sources primarily for support, which I am working on in the Research Methods module.
